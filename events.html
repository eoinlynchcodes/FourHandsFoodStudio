Rose expresses her creative cooking in pop up style events, sharing her idea of the future of food. 4 Hands dining experiences create conversation in health, farming practices, nutrition, regenerative agriculture and pre-digestion. Rose’s cooking is humble, natural and above all flavourful and nutritious.

Techniques to increase the nutrition of foods are used while also making them easier to digest, such as fermentation, sprouting, dehydration and slow cooking. This unique style, is mainly focused on plant based cooking but not solely, the use of ethically raised animal protein plays a major role in Rose’s idea of sustainable eating.

A deep respect for the entirety of an animal is paramount, traditional eating habits reflex this and Rose’s cooking shines a light on this by working with whole carcasses. 4 Hands belief is that we should be collectively stepping away from over consumption of animal protein, instead turning to eating smaller amounts less often of well raised produce from a known and trusted source.

Since 2014 Rose and Margaux have begun a transformation of their diets, both becoming great advocates of the health and emotional benefits of a more natural diet. Margaux shares her love of natural wine by sourcing from wine makers that work with a similar mindset to 4 Hands, in a world of overly processed food and drink, Margaux’s alcohol pairing is a celebration of raw and minimal intervention wine.

Alongside these rare wines stand selected spirits and beer from around Ireland, giving a one off experience. Non-alcoholic pairings are developed closely alongside the menu, made from the extensive 4 Hands larder of the seasons including fermented vegetable juices, tonics and kombucha, their complexity rivals that of their alcoholic counterparts. 4 Hands events are communal experiences, focused on bringing people together to share food and to enjoy the complexity and wonder of our environment, while being cared for by Rose and Margaux.
